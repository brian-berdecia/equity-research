Executive Summary

Bottom line: DNOW is a misunderstood story. The market keeps treating this like a pure-play oil price cyclical when the reality is the business has quietly transformed into a more diversified industrial distributor with a fortress balance sheet. My price target is $15.50 — roughly 24% upside from the current $12.47. The margin of safety here is real, not manufactured.

01
Balance Sheet Is The Story DNOW carries approximately $324M in cash and zero long-term debt. At current prices you're getting the operating business at a significant discount. That's rare in any sector.
02
Process Solutions Pivot The DistributionNOW Process Solutions segment is gaining traction. Revenue mix is shifting away from pure oil country tubular goods toward higher-margin process equipment and engineered products.
03
Buybacks As A Catalyst Management has been aggressive on share repurchases — roughly 10M shares reduced in the past 24 months. At these prices that's genuinely accretive. I expect this continues through 2026.
04
Valuation Disconnect At 6.8x EV/EBITDA and 14.2x earnings, DNOW trades at a 15–20% discount to distribution sector peers. The discount is too wide given the balance sheet quality and improving mix.
05
Near-Term Oil Volatility Is A Real Risk I won't pretend it isn't. If WTI crude sinks to $55–60/bbl territory for an extended period, DNOW's upstream-facing revenues take a hit. That's the primary downside scenario.
06
12-Month Thesis Three things unlock value: (1) continued buybacks, (2) further margin improvement in Process Solutions, and (3) any re-rating in small-cap energy distribution multiples. All three look achievable.

Company Overview

Business Description

DNOW Inc. is a leading industrial distribution company serving the oil, gas, and industrial sectors. Spun off from National Oilwell Varco (NOV) in May 2014, the company operates under the DistributionNOW and Process Solutions brands, providing pipes, valves, fittings, automation, process equipment, and related supply chain services.

The company operates across three geographic segments: United States, Canada, and International — with the U.S. segment contributing the majority of revenue. DNOW serves E&P companies, midstream operators, downstream refineries, and industrial end markets through approximately 190 service locations globally.

Here's what I find interesting: the "DistributionNOW" branding masks a genuine shift happening inside this business. The Process Solutions arm — which handles modular equipment, skid systems, and engineered process systems — is growing faster than the legacy pipe-and-valve distribution business, and it carries materially better margins. Management doesn't talk about this split loudly enough in my view.

My view: DNOW is not just a distributor. It's becoming a supply chain solutions provider. That distinction matters for valuation. Pure distributors trade at thin multiples. Solutions providers command a premium. The market hasn't caught up yet.
